# AIBioTrXiv versioning policy

AIBioTrXiv keeps a stable article record and stores every published version.

## Drafts before first publication

A manuscript is still **v1** while it is a draft or under first editorial review. Authors may edit the draft any number of times before the first publication. These draft edits do not create v2, v3, or any other public version number.

## First publication

The first public version, **v1**, requires formal submission, the initial US$5 processing payment where applicable, and editorial handling before publication.

## Post-publication versions

After v1 has been published, later public updates are handled as preserved article versions. Browse and topic pages show only the latest version, while older versions remain accessible from the article page.

## 12-hour correction window

After a new version is published, the author may correct that version within 12 hours without changing the version number. After the 12-hour window closes, the version is treated as fixed. Further changes must be published as the next version.

## Fee rule for later versions

Versions v2 through v15 do not require another PayPal payment and do not enter editorial screening by default. From **v16 onward**, each additional public version requires a US$5 platform maintenance fee before publication. These post-v15 versions still do not enter editorial review; the author remains fully responsible for the content.

## Version preservation

Every published version is preserved for timestamp traceability and credit history. Browse and topic pages show only the latest version. The public article page lists version links from oldest to newest.

## Moderation after publication

If a later version adds unlawful, abusive, fraudulent, unsafe, plagiarized, or misleading material, readers may report the article version. AIBioTrXiv may suspend the member account, unpublish the article, or take other moderation action. Suspension does not erase preserved records unless deletion is required by policy or law.
